10 Places You Should Visit In Guangzhou!

1.QingPing Market

A perfect place to visit in the early mornings. A great place to shop for traditional Chinese medicine. There are all kinds of herbs, seafood and exotic spices.

2.Shawan Ancient Town

A village that had remained unchanged. A beautiful place to see traditional Cantonese houses and

3.Yuyin Garden

Many miss its quieter corners. Look for hidden rockeries, bamboo groves, and shaded ponds where koi swim undisturbed. The design is intended for subtlety rather than grandeur—perfect for a contemplative walk.

4.Liwan Lake Park

Beyond a simple park, it's a hub for social life: locals dancing in groups, practicing martial arts, and playing traditional instruments. Visiting early in the morning is like stepping into a living painting.

5.Baiyun Mountain(White Cloud Mountain)

Explore paths that lead to hidden pavilions, ancient temples and quiet streams. A mixture of nature of nature and urban life.

6.Chen Clan Ancestrall Hall

Often overshadowed by more famous temples, this hall is a masterclass in Lingnan architecture: delicate wood carvings, intricate ceramics, and hidden courtyards. It’s not just a museum—it's an active community space with workshops.

7.Huacheng Square & Zhujiang New Town

Guangzhou's “financial district” has more than glass towers. Check out the reflective fountains at night, public art installations, and the view of Canton Tower from unusual angles that most tourists miss.

8.Liwan Bazaar / Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street

Not just shopping—wander the side alleys for street snacks that don't make it into the guidebooks. Think handmade dumplings, unique pastries, and snacks sold by vendors who have been there for decades.

9. Canton Tower Observation Deck

Experience the glass-bottomed skywalk and the LED light patterns at night. It's an immersive way to experience the city skyline differently.

10.Hualin Temple

Hualin is over 1,500 years old and features intricate stone carvings, courtyards, and a pond where koi fish reside.
